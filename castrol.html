Castrol Academy


Role: UX lead | Solution design | Concept design


Certain project details may have been omitted or obfuscated to comply with
NDA agreements and on-going project development.

Castrol is a subsidiary of BP and a leading brand of industrial and automotive lubricants. As a company of some magnitude operating worldwide, there emerged a desire to align all their employees across global geographies to the same standards of product knowledge and investment in the company ethos, thus creating a positive customer experience across the board, prompting development of the Castrol Academy learning platform.

Initial goals and outcomes defined

Castrol Academy was first developed in 2014, and designed to cater to different demographies around the world. The platform was designed for 'graceful degradation' owing to varied internet speeds and global devices at the time, and also sought to emphasise non-linear 'bitesize' learning for time-poor Castrol distributors and employees. I worked as UX lead on Castrol Academy from 2016, and continued the platforms growth, through concept design and UX work, responding to learner and administrator needs as it was gradually launched to thousands of new users around the world.

I often ideated on blackboards to identify questions and obstacles, and find creative solutions. Legacy systems integrating new features, new countries and learner types receiving appropriate content, and admin reporting were each addressed during my time on the Castrol Academy.

Back to basics

My task was to audit and improve both the learner and administrative aspects of Castrol Academy. I went back to basics to address the values and aims of the Academy and worked closely with the client to define the features, functions and purpose of the product as it continued to grow.

The circular device was developed for curriculum modules to encourage and emphasise a non-linear learning structure.

Designing the admin system

A crucial aspect of Castrol Academy I designed was a reporting system for administrators. The tool could be filtered from a global/country level, down to a regional, company and even individual level, which gave stakeholders and Castrol teams comprehensive oversight of learner usage, performance and engagement. The reporting system was permissions-based, with a hierarchy of 'super users' through to country and company administrators, who could get sight of learner progress within their remit via a user friendly, filterable reporting dashboard.

Left: An early wireframe concept which did not yet address all of the report data required by the admins. Right: The final report tool .

Tailoring the learner content

As new countries and learner types were added to Castrol Academy, new learning content was developed tailored to specific geographies and learners. I experimented with ideas and conceived of a tagging system which would replace the manual duplication and editing of learning content that had been used previously.

The tagging system categorised learning content based on country, industry, and language and added content to a users curriculum from a master 'content pool' when it matched these criteria of a users account.

An early draft of my content filtering logic, showing how content tagged with certain criteria would surface to relevant Academy users

Castrol Academy 2.0

I conducted on-going concept design and research into how Castrol Academy, could be modernised as a learning platform. This looked at both front-end and back-end solutions, for how legacy systems could be updated, or content could be completely migrated and overhauled to a new LMS or learning platform. The client requested several routes of research and design to present back to key stakeholders.

My concepts explored features such as on-boarding, leaderboards, 'unlockable' content and gamification rewards systems that would generate engagement from learners. These were brought to key stakeholders in Castrol to obtain buy-in with rationales as to why the Academy should be upgraded from its rudimentary legacy system.

Concept design: A series of wireframes were developed exploring how Castrol Academy would work with a more cohesive onboarding process and layout that adopted more contemporary app and LMS design patterns.

Introducing news and culture

In a later concept, the client requested examples of how Castrol Academy could integrate a news and culture hub into the learning platform, and also push UI design beyond the traditional green-red gradients of Castrol branding. I designed a concept that maintained focus on learning content and learner progress, but introduced a hierarchy of news and culture alongside this. This version of my concept design was signed off, and I built it as a front-end prototype to present to senior stakeholders.

Concept design: The client requested examples of how Castrol Academy learning to be integrated in with a news and culture hub.

Reskinned and mobile friendly

While it was resolved legacy systems would be maintained until 2021, I worked with my project team to make the platform responsive, and introduced a a light-CMS news hub which featured tagged and categorised content, which would surface depending on learner language, location and industry.

Providing on-boarding, guidance and on-going support

With each platform release and feature addition, I provided guidance and training for administrators and learners with training documentation and tutorial videos.

Results

Castrol Academy continues to grow from strength to strength, and is now used in over 20 countries around the world. My research and experience design work laid a foundation for functionality, feature building and design that is being progressively developed and released into 2021.

Functional specifications were developed for tech teams to help implement new features.
