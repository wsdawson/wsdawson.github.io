BP Carbon Calculator


Role: UX lead | UI design


Certain project details may have been omitted or obfuscated to comply with
NDA agreements and on-going project development.

Target Neutral is an arm of BP group aimed at reducing, replacing and neutralising carbon emissions. The carbon calculator is a digital product allowing users to tackle their carbon footprint by calculating their travel, and offsetting their journey emissions by contributing to one of Target Neutral's global green projects.

About the project

I operated as UX lead and digital designer from the outset of the project, working closely with the client, project manager, and developer, to craft a product that integrated into BP Target Neutral's existing back-end systems, along with the CRM platform Salesforce.

The initial project was scoped to integrate with BP's native app BPme, and with the Target Neutral website. This presented early challenges such as how different users would access the product and various system/API touchpoints. I spent early stages of the project working with developers to build a logic and architecture from which the product would function.

An early concept showing user entry points and where data would be captured.

Developing the concept

In tandem to establishing the technical parameters of the project, I considered the calculator from a user perspective and how their journey through the product would look. I developed a series of user flows, to show all potential routes through the product with the available options outlined by the client: car travel, aicraft travel, and manual tonnage.

I was keen to add a personal touch and give users a 'personal stake' in the carbon offsetting process by adding the ability to choose a project they wanted to contribute to once they'd calculated their cost.

This user flow shows each potential journey through the calculation tool, from entry through to checkout.

Prototyping and initial testing

Once the initial logic and user journey of the product had been established, I designed a series of interactive wireframes to be tested in a focus group. The purpose of the group was to gain initial insight to the premise we were working from, and also obtain attitudes and opinions towards carbon offsetting in general to help steer the messaging and deployment approach of the product.

Selected feedback from the carbon offsetting focus group.

The focus group used the protoyped app tool and offered feedback on initial functionality and experience, prompting elements such as the 'slider' function to be removed later. The client name was obfuscated until the end of the session, which divided some of the groups subsequent perception of the product.

Feedback loop and creative exploration

Working fast and iteratively with the product early on allowed me to make changes based on focus group and client feedback. For example, a slider function which had been introduced in first stage wireframes to add additional carbon emissions was removed, as it was deemed too complex. However certain functionality, such as contributing to specific projects could not be implemented, due to BP's careful vetting of specific global projects, and their inability to monitor to which project a given customers contributions would be sent.

Left: I wrote example copy into the wireframes, which was annotated with the intent and purpose of the message, this was developed with a senior copywriter.
Right: The developed messaging. Research had shown that carbon footprint was popular and well-understood vernacular.

UI development

Using a combination of atomic design principles and the BP brand guidelines a series of templates and a pattern library was developed from the wireframes. The styling was kept clean and minimal so as not to distract from the task-orientated nature of the calculator. BP's brand typeface Univers Sans, along with the primary green of the brand was used to effect to dictate much of the design.

Their was a slight difference in styling for the BPme app, which utilised a gradient colour scheme and used Google's material design Android icons rather than BP-specific icons.

Handover and CMS build

The UI was developed to sit seamlessly within the inverted L-shape of the Target Neutral website header and vertical navigation. My templates along with the pattern library and functional documentation were handed over to BP's tech-team to integrate to their CMS and plug-in calculation data from ERM. I worked closely with the both my project team and client team during this handover period to ensure a seamless deployment.
