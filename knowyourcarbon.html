Know your Carbon Footprint


Role: UX lead | UI design

Know Your Carbon Footprint is a digital product that lets you calculate your personal carbon emissions in a fun and engaging way and pledge to reduce them. I led the UX process for this project in collaboration with a Creative Director and Copy Lead to develop a visual style and tone of voice that married the BP brand with a unique digital experience.

Initial goals and outcomes defined

In line with the clients ambitions, my goals for this project were to create a concise and enjoyable carbon calculator tool, that would create awareness of carbon emissions and BP's role in addressing them. Audiences would be incentivised to share the tool on their social channels to generate awareness.

The final tool was launched at the One Young World event, and subsequently developed to be accessible online by the general public.

UX & Design challenges

This tool was developed at pace, keeping to a short timeframe of 2 months for a go live at the One Young World Event. During this time I scoped and ran iterative discovery phases, and collaborated with a product team to design, build and test the calculator.

The user experience had to be as concise as possible, while still enjoyable, as audiences would only be available during event intervals to walk about the stands in the atrium.

The client was also keen to obtain audience buy-in for their future carbon reducing projects, and so considerations around data capture, GDPR were made to balance the enjoyment of the tool with data-capture and client requirements.

Low fidelity ideation permitted a rapid development of concepts and layouts.

The process

I developed experience flows for two distinct use cases; event audiences and non-event audiences. Rapid ideation and co-design with the client took place to effectively define the structure of the calculator, content and functionality.

I also worked with the client and a Copy Lead to develop a series of questions about personal consumption under the categories of Home, Travel, Food, and Purchases. The calculator algorithm was created by ERM based on this work.

Early flows explored entry points, and how an audience might be introduced to, and interact with the digital tool on different devices.

Scamping the event app experience

From the ideation phase, the event app was conceived for iPad tablets, to be carried by BP representatives, who would offer users the chance to use the calculator and make a pledge.

Interactivity and enjoyment was essential for the event experience, so the app was tailored to prompt the user to be photographed by the BP representative at the BP stand following their calculation result.

If consented by the user, they would be added to a live 'pledge wall' screen at the event, and a set of social assets would be auto-generated and emailed to the user, to share and raise awareness on their channels.

As concepts came to fruition we created clickable prototypes with greyscale wireframes to communicate concepts, UX flows and rudimentary UI with clients.

Wireframing

I evolved the wireframe process from white board ideation into clickable greyscale prototypes. These were iteratively guerilla tested with friends and colleagues owing to time constraints. I also collaborated with the development team on the functional specifications to refine and optimise how calculations, APIs and email generation would plug-in to the tool, along with defining validation parameters.

UI design was developed to balance the illustrative flourishes of the event branding with the colours and typography of the BP brand. The app was also developed to be AA accessible, so an unobtrusive, well contrasted identity was developed.

UI design

A distinctive illustration style had been created for the BP event stand, which I incorporated into the UI development of the tool for consistency. A clean and clear interface was developed which used the BP palette and illustrative flourishes sparingly, and made progression through the calculation questions swift, task-oriented and enjoyable.

Results

The calculator was well received and used by over 400 delegates at One Young World. The website version of the tool also received over 22,000 hits as social media awareness sprung up in the wake of the event.
