Unite Students is the UK's leading PBSA (purpose built student accommodation) provider with around 75,000 students staying each year in 172 properties around the country. I joined the company as a Senior UX/UI Designer to help lead creatively in the companies digital transformation, own the digital brand/UI, and optimise experience across the customer lifecycle.

Design System and UI refresh

The design system is a cloud-based resource which enables designers to review and grab resources directly for their projects from a single source of truth.

Creating the Design System

One of my first actions when joining Unite Students was to refresh the design of key property pages and create more cohesive workflows and consistencies in the digital brand. Without a centralised 'source of truth' I identified inconsistencies of UI and design patterns across internal and customer-facing applications and proposed to resolve this.

My solution was to create and deploy a comprehensive design system that could serve as a reference tool, onboarding function, and source of truth for designers and stakeholders, allowing for harmonious UI design across digital products. I created corresponding master libraries in Adobe XD (and later Figma) so that the design team could intuitively access and reference accurate design components for their work.

Master component libraries were built into Adobe XD and later Figma to provide teams with the correct UI references for their projects.

Design updates were easier to implement and govern once the principles and re-usable components had been built into a system.

Measuring success

Since deploying the design system and UI component libraries the previous bottle-necks at QA and artworking stages have been drastically reduced across the project teams. The systems continue to be maintained and updated when new iconography or styles are proposed, ensuring designers, stakeholders and teams can create consistent UI in-keeping with the digital brand.

Map listings UX/UI design

The Project

Students arriving at Unite Students properties come from all over the world and creating a sense of proximity and orientation for them prior to visiting or booking is an important factor in their exploration and conversion journey. The development of the Map UI consisted of workshops, focus groups and competitor analysis of existing map design patterns to arrive at a feature that intuitively meets the needs of customers and allows them the freedom to explore properties, travel routes and local amenities without leaving the site and jumping into a comparable tool such as google maps.

Each detail and clickable state of the map was outlined to determine how a user could intuitively interact with it.

The map was released in phases, with layers of more advanced function and UI added in each sprint. This is an example of the Points of Interest toggle key that displays nearby amenities to our properties

Measuring success

The map was tested with a small pool of students and was well received. Feedback suggested additional features and suggestions that they would find useful such as routing times between properties and universities, which were added into a subsequent release. As with all new features the map continues to be iterated upon and features tested with audiences.

Room selector feature

The room selector comprises a visually engaging digital floorplan in which customers can review, select and book rooms.

The Project

The primary route to booking with Unite Students is selecting a room type (e.g. Ensuite or Studio) from a set of listings. The customer is then randomly allocated a room within the property and has no say on where this is. It often results in room move requests and additional administration when the customer arrives at the property and creates dissatisfaction and resource drain on on-site teams. Additionally, cultural factors play a part in room allocation and, for example, many Chinese students who comprise a large market for Unite Students associate the number 4 with bad luck, and do not like staying on floors/rooms including this figure.

The proposed solution was to empower the customer and give them agency when booking through an intuitive Room selector feature. This comprised of an interactive floorplan that allowed users to navigate through properties and floors, observing available rooms and book one they found most suitable. The visualisation of the property tested well with students in workshops and was developed for ten properties in an initial deployment.

The project evolved out of some comprehensive competitor reviews around what other providers did and did not offer customers.

The stages of room selection, now including a group booking feature where a user may select multiple rooms and share with friends.

The feature was designed to scale seamlessly across all device viewports, with the UI adapting for optimal user experience.

Measuring Success

The room selector was deployed for 10 properties as a test pool to gauge reaction and interest. It proved popular, with prospective customers enjoying the tangible glimpse of the property layout, along with the freedom to choose their rooms. However the lack of group booking functionality was raised, and this was a feature I proceeded to develop for the next phase of release on the room selector, which would see it rolled out to additional properties in the Unite Students portfolio.
